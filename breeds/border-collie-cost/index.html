Border Collie-specific cost drivers

  • Incredible energy — under-exercise causes behavioral issues
  • High incidence of hip dysplasia
  • Mental stimulation essential for well-being

Insurance fit

Border Collies often benefit from pet insurance given documented health risks in the breed. Early enrollment locks in coverage before conditions develop, especially for joint issues and cardiac concerns common to the line.

Ways to manage cost

  • Budget for dog daycare or trainer if owner works long hours
  • Daily intensive exercise (herding, agility, fetch)
  • Consider preventive hip scoring

FAQ

How much does a Border Collie cost per year?

Border Collies typically cost $2,000 per year in ongoing expenses including food, preventive care, grooming, and emergency fund contributions. Costs vary by location, insurance, and individual health.

What is the lifetime cost of a Border Collie?

Over a typical 13-year lifespan, including a $1,500 purchase price, a Border Collie will cost roughly $21,700–$33,450. This assumes standard preventive care and no major emergencies.

Is a Border Collie expensive to insure?

Pet insurance premiums vary, but Border Collies often qualify for breed-specific rates. Early enrollment typically offers lower premiums and better coverage options.
