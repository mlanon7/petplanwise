Pembroke Welsh Corgi-specific cost drivers

  • Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD). The long-spine + short-leg build predisposes to disc problems. Surgery for severe cases runs $4,000–$8,000.
  • Degenerative myelopathy (DM). Pembrokes are the #1 breed for DM — a progressive spinal-cord disease. DNA test ($100) at adoption identifies risk; affected dogs need mobility support and eventually carts ($300–$1,000).
  • Obesity drives joint disease. Corgis gain weight easily and the extra pounds amplify back/hip strain. Lean body condition is the single biggest cost lever.
  • Hip dysplasia. Higher rate than the small-breed average. Severe surgery $3,000–$6,000.

Insurance for Corgis

Corgi premiums average $30–$55/month. Reasonable fit because of orthopedic + neurologic risk. Pick a policy that covers spinal surgery without a per-condition cap.

Ways to save

  • Run the DM DNA test at adoption — informs long-term planning.
  • Use ramps for car/couch from day one — reduces IVDD risk meaningfully.
  • Strict food measurement; treats included in daily calories.
  • Adopt — Corgi rescue groups (Mayflower Pembroke Welsh Corgi Club rescue, East Coast Corgi Rescue) often have young dogs.

FAQ

How much does a Pembroke Welsh Corgi cost per year?

$1,500–$3,400. Joint health and weight management drive most of the variance.

Do Pembroke Corgis shed a lot?

Yes — heavy double coat blows twice a year. Daily brushing during seasonal coat blow is non-negotiable.

Pembroke vs Cardigan — what's cheaper?

Pembroke is more popular and generally $300–$800 less to purchase. Lifetime costs are similar.
