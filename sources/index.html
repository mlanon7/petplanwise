Bibliography

Sources used

A consolidated list of the data, surveys, and guidelines we draw on. Where individual numbers come from a specific source, we link inline on the relevant page.

Industry & ownership data

  • AVMA — U.S. Pet Ownership and Demographics Sourcebook
  • APPA — National Pet Owners Survey
  • Banfield Pet Hospital — State of Pet Health
  • ASPCA — pet care cost guidance
  • Synchrony / CareCredit — Lifetime of Care studies (cats and dogs)

Veterinary cost & pricing

  • U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — CPI for Veterinary Services
  • AVDC — American Veterinary Dental College
  • VECCS — Veterinary Emergency & Critical Care Society

AAHA / AAFP veterinary practice guidelines

  • AAHA 2022 Canine Vaccination Guidelines — used for core vs non-core dog vaccines
  • AAHA/AAFP 2020 Feline Vaccination Guidelines — used for cat vaccine logic

Veterinary research & specialty centers

  • Cornell Feline Health Center — peer-reviewed cat health information
  • Cornell Riney Canine Health Center
  • OFA — Orthopedic Foundation for Animals
  • JAVMA / AVMA Open — peer-reviewed veterinary research

Behavior & breed-trait research

  • C-BARQ — Canine Behavioral Assessment & Research Questionnaire — UPenn Vet (Hsu & Serpell, 2003 and ongoing). The validated owner-survey instrument used in dozens of peer-reviewed canine-behavior studies; the source for breed-level aggregate scores on attachment, stranger reactivity, trainability, and energy on our breed comparison page.
  • Morrill et al. (2022) — Ancestry-inclusive dog genomics challenges popular breed stereotypes — Science. The 2,000-dog study showing breed explains only ~9% of behavioral variation between individual dogs. Cited site-wide as the basis for "breed tendencies, not individual predictions" framing.
  • American Temperament Test Society (ATTS) — public breed pass-rate data used to sanity-check temperament claims.
  • AKC breed standards — function statements (Working, Herding, Sporting, etc.) used for protective-instinct context.
  • CFA — Cat Fanciers' Association breed standards and TICA breed standards — cat trait baselines.

Pet insurance & consumer guidance

  • NAPHIA — North American Pet Health Insurance Association
  • NAIC — pet insurance model law and consumer guidance

Government & regulatory

  • CDC — Healthy Pets, Healthy People
  • FDA-CVM — Center for Veterinary Medicine
  • BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey — pets, pet products, services

Where individual numbers come from a specific source, that source is linked inline on the relevant page. The CSV files under /assets/data/csv/ are the underlying dataset and are themselves auditable.
